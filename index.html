Leadership Perspective

Guiding education with responsible AI innovation

AI stands at an inflection point where generative AI fundamentally reimagines how we research, learn, and grow. This moment calls for both excitement and responsibility.

Read the full perspective

We are at a moment where every institution must determine the right balance of enthusiasm and caution for their use of AI. For some, the rapid pace of AI development suggests exercising restraint, and for others AI represents an opportunity to break down traditional barriers in education for teachers and learners alike.

That dynamic is exactly why we created the Education AI Toolkit — to help institutions navigate their unique AI journey. As someone who grew up in the classroom as a teacher and administrator, I believe the best innovations in education come when they are grounded in real educator needs and informed by responsible practice.

This toolkit brings together frameworks for planning and implementation, real-world stories from institutions leading the way, and practical guidance for building AI-powered experiences. Whether you are just beginning to explore AI or scaling it across your institution, this resource is designed to meet you where you are.

— Matt Jubelirer, Vice President, Microsoft Education

AI Integration Phases

Three phases of AI adoption in education

Phase 1

Human-led work with AI assistance

Educators and staff use AI tools to support everyday tasks like drafting communications or generating lesson ideas. Work remains human-directed, with AI assisting in efficiency and preparation.

Phase 2

Human-guided, agent-supported teams

AI systems take on defined, narrow responsibilities. An AI-supported service may respond to common student inquiries and escalate complex cases to staff for review.

Phase 3

Human-directed, agent-operated workflows

Educators define goals and parameters while AI systems execute portions of workflows under clear governance. High-stakes decisions remain human responsibilities.
