AI Integration Phases

Three phases of AI adoption in education

Institutions move through overlapping phases, with progress varying by function and role. What matters most is intentional adoption aligned to institutional values.

Phase 1

Human-led work with AI assistance

Educators and staff use AI tools to support everyday tasks like drafting communications or generating lesson ideas. Work remains human-directed, with AI assisting in efficiency and preparation.

Phase 2

Human-guided, agent-supported teams

AI systems take on defined, narrow responsibilities. An AI-supported service may respond to common student inquiries and escalate complex cases to staff for review.

Phase 3

Human-directed, agent-operated workflows

Educators define goals and parameters while AI systems execute portions of workflows under clear governance. High-stakes decisions remain human responsibilities.

Implementation Roadmap

AI implementation in five steps

A practical framework for education leaders to move from exploration to measurable impact.

1

Exploration & Planning

Engage your community, define your goals, and identify educational outcomes AI can enhance.

2

Data & Infrastructure

Strengthen governance, break down data silos, and prepare your infrastructure for AI readiness.

3

Pilot Implementation

Offer professional learning, run a controlled pilot, and gather feedback from educators and staff.

4

Scale & Optimize

Introduce AI-driven administration tools, expand successful pilots, and refine based on feedback.

5

Evaluate & Review
